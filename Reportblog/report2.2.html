Auctions

Auctions on UNXD are best price auctions, meaning the collector with the highest bid at the close of the auction will win.

These auctions occur during an auction period when collectors can bid. At the close of the bidding period, the collector with the highest bid wins.

To ensure collectors have a chance to reply if they are outbid, a higher bid placed in the last 10 minutes of an auction extends the auction time by 15 minutes. If no new higher bid is placed by the end of the 15 minutes, then the auction ends.

All higher bids must conform to the bidding schedule to be valid.

To Summarize:

  • Before Auction Start - Collectors can view the NFT.
  • After Auction Start - Bidding is open and collectors can place higher bids according to the bidding schedule.
  • Within 5 Minutes of Auction End - If a valid higher bid is placed within the last 5 minutes, the auction is extended by 15 minutes.
  • After the Auction End - The highest bid wins the auction. The funds are moved from the smart contract and into the treasury to be dispersed to the creator(s).

Bidding

Before bidding, ensure you have a spending balance that is equal or greater than your whole desired bidding budget or you will have to top up your Spending Balance

To bid, visit the page of an NFT auction in which you want to participate. The page will list the reserve price or current highest bid. To bid, click on "Place Bid" on the page of the NFT you wish to buy.

This will open a modal where you can place a bid on the item. To be a valid bid, it must not exceed your Spending Balance and be valid according to the Bidding Schedule.

If you submit a valid bid, your MataMask will open a window asking you to approve a transaction that will move the bid amount from Spending Balance to a Reserve Balance that is locked up in this bid. All bids are final and paid in exchange for the NFT if it is the highest bid at the close of the auction.

Submitting a bid will take some time because a record of the transaction is being placed on the Polygon blockchain. Because multiple bids can be placed at once, there is a chance that you are outbid before the bid is settled.

Take, for example, a case where Bidder A submits a bid for 11 wETH and Bidder B submits a bid for 12 wETH in that order on an item currently at 10 wETH. Bidder A will have the highest bid despite being lower than Bidder B because of the order in which the transactions were submitted.

When Bidder A's transaction was approved, it became became the new highest bid when it was confirmed. However, while Bidder B's 12 wETH bid was over 10% of the highest bid when submitted, it is not 10% higher than Bidder A's 11 wETH new highest bid approved before it. In this case, Bidder B's transaction would fail as not a high enough bid.

Should you be outbid, your Reserve Balance locked up for the bid will be returned to your Spending Balance. This Spending Balance can be used for making more bids, or transferred back to your wallet.

Biding Schedule

To keep auctions moving and prevent bidders from being outbid by small amounts, bids must proceed in increments according to a bidding schedule below.

Current Bid Min Bid
Reserve Price @>= Reserve Price
>= Reserve Price & < 3,333 wETH @>= High Bid + 10%
>= 3,333 wETH >= High Bid + 333 wETH
